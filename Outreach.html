Classroom and public presentations

I have delivered presentations to dozens of elementary school classrooms, from Kindergarten to Grade 7. I am always open to presentation requests, either in-person or via zoom; feel free to reach out if you are an educator looking for a guest speaker. I have presented on a range of topics related to evolutionary biology, genetics, and bird biology, and I am happy to customize my presentations for different age ranges or topics of interest.

Recent presentation topics include:

  • All about to Owls (Presented to grade 1 classrooms)
  • Bird Adaptations (Presented to: Kindergarten through grade 5)
  • Introduction to Evolution (Presented to: grades 4 through 7)
  • Birds in the Neighbourhood: Introduction to the birds of Vancouver (Presented to: grades 1 through 4)
  • Making Sense of Bird Senses: How Birds Perceive the World (Public presentation at Vancouver Bird Week 2020)
  • Feathered Jewels in our Backyard: the birds of Keats Island (Public presentation via Keats Island Conservation Society, 2025)

Field Trips

I have led dozens of nature tours and field trips for a variety of audiences ranging from families with young children to professional biologists. I love fostering connections between people and nature, whether it is taking children on their first trip to the intertidal zone or helping budding ornithologists refine their bird survey techniques. During my undergraduate studies, I was the coordinator for the Stanley Park Ecology Society bird monitoring program, where I led monthly bird walks. I have also run nature interpretation stations and led birding tours for conference attendees, and I lead nature tours for the Keats Island Conservation Society. Feel free to reach out if you would like to arrange a bird/nature tour for an event.
