Classroom presentations

I have delivered presentations to dozens of elementary school classrooms, from Kindergarten to Grade 7. I am always open to presentation requests, either in-person or via zoom; feel free to reach out if you are an educator looking for a guest speaker. I have presented on a range of topics related to evolutionary biology, genetics, and bird biology, and I am happy to customize my presentations for different age ranges or topics of interest.

Recent presentation topics include:

  • Making Sense of Bird Senses: How Birds Perceive the World (Public presentation at Vancouver Bird Week 2020)
  • All about to Owls (Presented to 2 Grade 1 classrooms in 2023)
  • Bird Adaptations (Presented to: 3 Kindergarten, 1 Grade 1/2, 2 Grade 2/3, 1 Grade 4, 1 Grade 4/5, and 1 Grade 5 classroom in 2023)
  • Introduction to Evolution (Presented to: 1 Grade 6/7 classroom in 2023; 3 Grade 4/5 and 3 Grade 6/7 classrooms in 2022)
  • Birds in the Neighbourhood: Introduction to the birds of Vancouver (Presented to: 2 Grade 1 and 2 Grade 3/4 classrooms in 2022)

Field Trips

I have led dozens of nature tours and field trips for a variety of audiences ranging from families with young children to professional biologists. I love fostering connections between people and nature, whether it is taking children on their first trip to the intertidal zone or helping budding ornithologists refine their bird survey techniques. During my undergraduate studies, I was the coordinator for the Stanley Park Ecology Society bird monitoring program, where I led monthly bird walks. I have also run nature interpretation stations and led birding tours for conference attendees. Feel free to reach out if you would like to arrange a bird/nature tour for an event.
